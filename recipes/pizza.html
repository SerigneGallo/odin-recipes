Pizza

Description

Pizza Margherita showcases the quintessential flavors of Italy with its simple, fresh ingredients. It features a base of tender dough topped with vibrant tomato sauce, slices of fresh mozzarella, and whole basil leaves.

A drizzle of olive oil is added before baking in a hot oven, creating a crispy, charred crust with a gooey, melted cheese topping. The combination of tangy tomatoes, creamy mozzarella, and aromatic basil makes this pizza a timeless favorite.

Ingredients

  • dough - enough for one large pizza
  • sauce - 1/2 cup, preferably made from San Marzano tomatoes
  • Fresh mozzarella cheese - 200 grams, sliced or torn into pieces
  • Fresh basil leaves - a handful
  • Extra virgin olive oil - for drizzling
  • Salt - to taste

Steps

  1. Preheat the Oven: Set your oven to its highest temperature (450-500°F or 230-260°C). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven to preheat as well.
  2. Prepare the Dough: If you're using store-bought or premade dough, let it come to room temperature. This makes it easier to stretch without tearing. On a floured surface, gently stretch the dough into a round shape using your hands or a rolling pin, aiming for even thickness.
  3. Add the Tomato Sauce: Spoon the tomato sauce onto the center of your stretched dough. Using the back of the spoon, spread the sauce evenly, leaving a small border around the edges for the crust.
  4. Add the Cheese and Basil: Distribute slices of fresh mozzarella evenly over the sauce.Scatter fresh basil leaves on top of the cheese. You can tear larger leaves to distribute the flavor more evenly.
  5. Drizzle with Olive Oil: Lightly drizzle extra virgin olive oil over the pizza. This adds flavor and helps achieve a golden color.
  6. Bake the Pizza: Transfer the pizza to the preheated oven (or onto the pizza stone if using). Bake for 10-15 minutes or until the crust is puffy and golden and the cheese is bubbly and slightly browned. If your oven has a broiler, you can broil the pizza for the last minute to get a more charred top.
  7. Serving: Once baked, remove the pizza from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. This allows the cheese to set slightly, making it easier to cut. Slice and serve hot.
